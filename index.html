Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) is a key in diabetes care and management asmore traditional measures of glucose control, such as single point estimates ofmonthly/weekly glucose averages are note able to provide information about day to daychanges in sugar levels. Although few computational packages exist for summarymeasure computation, non of them provide explicit guidance on how to deal with CGMtime series data. Thus, a computational package with user-friendly pipeline calledglucoCheckwas developed in python to enable overall analysis of CGM data - from datacleaning, imputation to clustering and extraction of glucose variability indices.

Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) is a key component in treatment and self-care strategies development of diabetic individuals .Today, various wearable and implantable devices are available on the market (..) that can track continuous glucose levels throughout several days so that trends and patterns of glucose dys-regulation could be summarised and timely decisions about necessary medications and lifestyle changes could be made. Although this system has a huge clinical application -- management of diabetes, it is also an important research platform, which generates a considerable amount of data: a single sensor can provide up to 14 weeks of glucose readings, which are reported every e.g. 1/5/15min across $N$ number of individuals, resulting in time series data. In research settings this data can be used e.g.: (a) to better understand the onset of pre-diabetes so that new policies aiming at prevention of progression to type II diabetes could be developed; (b) study the impact of various diets on sugar levels; or (c) to better understand how glucose regulation changes during the course of entire pregnancy and better understand the onset of pregnancy related (gestational) diabetes.
